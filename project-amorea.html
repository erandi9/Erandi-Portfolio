Amorea

Wedding invitation and guest management platform designed to help couples create, customize, and share their digital invitations with ease.

Final Product

Project Overview

Summary

The experience focuses on simplicity, emotional warmth, and clear guidance supporting users as they design their invitation, manage RSVPs, and keep track of guest details without friction.

Role

Product Designer & UX Lead, responsible for shaping the onboarding experience, defining core flows, refining layout and interaction patterns, and crafting emotionally resonant microcopy across the platform.

Team

A small, focused team composed of the developer, collaborating closely to define features, refine flows, and bring the product to life.

Timeline

Ongoing multi phase project, including discovery, flow definition, onboarding design, iterative prototyping, and continuous refinement as new features are developed.

Context & Problem

Background

Planning a wedding involves dozens of moving parts, and many couples struggle to keep track of invitations, RSVPs, and guest details across multiple tools. Amorea was created to simplify this experience by offering a single place where couples can design their digital invitation, share it with guests, and manage responses effortlessly. Because the product was being built from the ground up, there was no existing structure, flow, or content model, everything needed to be defined, from the onboarding to the core interactions and the emotional tone of the experience.

The Challenge

The main challenge is designing a platform that feels simple, warm, and intuitive while the product itself is still taking shape. Because Amorea is being built feature by feature, the structure, flows, and content model need to remain flexible and scalable. Couples require clear guidance without feeling overwhelmed, and the experience must support both the emotional tone of a wedding journey and the practical complexity of guest management. With a very small team, just the developer and me, every decision must balance feasibility, clarity, and longn term growth. This means continuously refining the Information Architecture, shaping new flows as they emerge, and crafting microcopy that supports users through an evolving product.

Information Architecture

Amorea started without an existing structure, so the Information Architecture had to be defined from the ground up. Because the product is still evolving, the IA needs to remain flexible while giving users a clear, intuitive path through invitation creation, guest management, and RSVP tracking. My work has focused on establishing a foundation that supports both the emotional tone of a wedding platform and the practical tasks couples need to complete.
I organized the core flows around the natural steps couples take, creating their invitation, customizing it, sharing it, and managing guest responses.

As new features are added, I continuously refine the hierarchy, naming, and grouping of content to keep the experience simple and predictable. This includes shaping the onboarding logic, defining how information is surfaced across screens, and ensuring that each new feature fits into a coherent structure rather than becoming an isolated add on. Because the team is just the developer and me, IA decisions happen iteratively and collaboratively. Each new feature requires revisiting the structure, adjusting flows, and validating that the experience still feels clear, warm, and easy to navigate. The result is an IA that grows with the product while staying grounded in user needs and emotional clarity.

Reflection

Designing Amorea has been a reminder of how different it feels to build a product from the ground up, especially with such a small team. Because the platform is still evolving, I’ve had to stay flexible, constantly adjusting flows, refining the IA, and reshaping decisions as new needs appear. It’s taught me the value of designing for clarity even when the full picture isn’t defined yet, and how important it is to create foundations that can grow without becoming heavy or confusing.

Working so closely with the developer has also reinforced how essential communication is in early stage products. Every feature requires alignment, negotiation, and quick iteration, and I’ve learned to balance emotional tone, usability, and feasibility in real time. As the product continues to grow, I’m becoming more intentional about documenting decisions, anticipating future complexity, and protecting the simplicity that makes Amorea feel warm and approachable.
