CULTURATE

Designing the digital infrastructure for a global cultural conference through innovative information architecture and user centric event experience.

Final Product

Project Overview

Summary

Mobile app designed to centralize all museums in Buenos Aires, Argentina, giving users a simple and engaging way to discover cultural spaces across the city.

Role

UX Designer, responsible for shaping the user experience across research, flow definition, interaction design, and prototyping

Team

A small, multidisciplinary team composed of two UX designers, one graphic designer, and one marketing partner.

Timeline

Multi phase project including research, concept development, prototyping, user testing, and iterative refinement.

Context & Problem

Background

Buenos Aires has one of the richest museum ecosystems in Latin America, but information about these spaces is scattered across individual websites, outdated directories, and inconsistent social media pages. For locals and tourists who love cultural experiences, finding accurate details location, hours, exhibitions, ticketing, often requires jumping between multiple sources. CULTURATE was created to bring all museums into one centralized mobile platform, making cultural exploration easier, more intuitive, and more enjoyable. The app allows users to search through filters, use a search bar, explore an interactive map that detects their location, save favorites, read and leave reviews, and book tickets directly from the platform.

The Challenge

The main challenge was designing an experience that could organize a large amount of diverse museum information while still feeling simple, modern, and easy to navigate. Users needed multiple ways to discover museums, by interest, proximity, or name, without feeling overwhelmed by options. The platform also had to support richer interactions like reviews, favorites, and ticket booking, all within a clean and intuitive mobile flow. Because the cultural landscape of Buenos Aires is constantly evolving, the product needed a flexible structure that could scale as new museums, features, and content types were added. Balancing clarity, depth, and scalability became central to shaping the UX strategy.

Goals & Responsibilities

Primary Goals

  • Centralize all museums in Buenos Aires into a single, easy to navigate mobile platform.
  • Create multiple discovery paths filters, search, and an interactive map to help users find museums that match their interests.
  • Design a simple, modern experience that makes cultural exploration accessible to both locals and tourists.
  • Support richer engagement through features like favorites, reviews, and ticket booking.
  • Build a scalable structure that can grow as new museums, categories, and features are added.
  • Establish a visual and interaction language that feels culturally aligned with Buenos Aires.

My Responsibilities

  • Conducted user research to understand how people search for museums and what information they value most.
  • Defined the Information Architecture and core flows for search, filters, map exploration, museum details, favorites, reviews, and ticket booking.
  • Designed wireframes and interactive prototypes to validate usability and refine the experience.
  • Collaborated with the second UX designer to align on flows, patterns, and interaction logic.
  • Worked closely with the graphic designer to integrate branding, color, and visual identity into the UI.
  • Partnered with the marketing team member to ensure content, tone, and positioning matched user motivations and cultural engagement goals.
  • Iterated on the design through testing and feedback, refining clarity, navigation, and overall usability.

Information Architecture

CULTURATE required an Information Architecture that could organize a large and diverse set of museums while still feeling simple and intuitive for users exploring cultural spaces across Buenos Aires. Because the app supports multiple discovery paths, filters, search, and an interactive map, the IA needed to balance flexibility with clarity, ensuring users could always find what they were looking for without cognitive overload.

The structure was built around the three primary ways people naturally search for museums: by interest, by name, and by proximity. This led to a navigation model centered on Search, Filters, and Map, each offering a different entry point into the same unified museum database. From there, the museum detail pages became the core content hub, housing essential information such as hours, exhibitions, location, reviews, and ticket booking options.

To support deeper engagement, the IA also included personalized spaces like Favorites and Reviews, giving users a sense of ownership and continuity as they explored the city’s cultural offerings. Throughout the process, we refined naming, grouping, and hierarchy to keep the experience lightweight and predictable, even as new museums or categories were added.

Reflection

Working on CULTURATE showed me how essential it is to design for clarity when a product brings together a large amount of diverse information. Centralizing all the museums in Buenos Aires into a single mobile experience required constant negotiation between depth and simplicity, ensuring users could explore freely without ever feeling overwhelmed or lost. It pushed me to think more strategically about how people search, how they make decisions, and how to support different discovery behaviors within one coherent structure.

Collaborating with a multidisciplinary team reinforced the value of cross disciplinary alignment. Each perspective, UX, visual design, and marketing, shaped the product in a different way, and balancing these inputs made me more intentional about communication, documentation, and shared decision making. As the app evolved, I learned to iterate quickly, validate assumptions early, and design with scalability in mind, knowing that new museums, categories, and features would continue to expand the experience. This project strengthened my ability to create systems that feel simple on the surface but are supported by thoughtful structure underneath, an approach that has become central to how I design complex, content rich products.
