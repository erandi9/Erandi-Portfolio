CULTURATE

CULTURATE is a mobile web application designed to help users discover museums across Buenos Aires, Argentina, and plan cultural experiences in a simple and accessible way.

The platform centralizes museum information and allows users to explore options based on their interests, location, and availability without requiring account creation or upfront payment.

The goal was to reduce friction in the discovery process and encourage users to engage with cultural experiences more easily.

View Final Product

Problem

Exploring cultural activities in a large city like Buenos Aires can be challenging:

  • Information about museums is scattered across different sources
  • Users struggle to find options that match their interests or location
  • Existing platforms often require account creation or payment before users can explore
  • Lack of centralized, user friendly tools reduces engagement

This creates friction in the discovery process and limits access to cultural experiences.

My Role

UX/UI Designer collaborating within a cross functional team.

  • Worked alongside 2 UX designers, a developer, a marketing specialist, and a visual designer
  • Contributed to user research, ideation, and design decisions
  • Created wireframes and interactive prototypes in Figma
  • Participated in usability testing and iterative improvements
  • Collaborated using Notion to track tasks and ensure alignment across the team

Approach

The team followed a user centered design process focused on reducing friction and improving accessibility.

  • Defined requirements and aligned on product goals
  • Conducted user research to understand behaviors and expectations
  • Created personas and user journeys to identify pain points
  • Developed low, mid, and high fidelity prototypes
  • Conducted usability testing at each stage to validate decisions

Key Decisions

  • Removed mandatory onboarding to allow users to explore the platform immediately
  • Delayed monetization (ticket purchases) to reduce friction and build user trust
  • Designed multiple discovery paths:
    • Search
    • Filters
    • Category navigation
    • Location based map
  • Integrated geolocation to surface nearby museums and improve relevance
  • Included reviews and comments to support decision making

Solution

The final product enables users to:

  • Discover museums through multiple intuitive pathways
  • Explore options based on location, interests, and categories
  • View detailed information and user feedback
  • Save favorite museums and manage tickets (for registered users)
  • Navigate a seamless experience without unnecessary barriers

The platform balances accessibility for new users with additional functionality for returning users.

Validation & Iteration

Usability testing was conducted across all stages of the design process.

  • Tested low, mid, and high fidelity prototypes with multiple users
  • Identified usability issues and friction points early
  • Iterated designs based on real user feedback
  • Refined flows and interactions to improve clarity and usability

The final high fidelity prototype was validated with users, resulting in a more intuitive and effective experience.

Impact

  • Reduced barriers to entry by eliminating mandatory onboarding
  • Improved discoverability through multiple navigation paths
  • Increased usability through continuous testing and iteration
  • Created a flexible platform that supports both exploration and conversion

Reflection

This project reinforced the value of collaboration and continuous validation.
Working within a multidisciplinary team required clear communication, alignment, and shared ownership of decisions. Iterative testing played a key role in refining the experience and ensuring that the final solution addressed real user needs.
