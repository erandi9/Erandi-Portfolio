Project Overview

Summary

Redesign of PepsiCo’s global intranet, a platform used across regions and business units. My main focus was improving the Information Architecture and overall layout to make the experience more intuitive and user friendly.

Role

UX Designer, Information Architecture, navigation redesign, taxonomy refinement, research support, interaction design.

Team

Worked alongside a UX Architect, UX Designers, Product Managers, Engineering teams, Content/Communications partners, and the designer responsible for the brand redesign.

Timeline

Multi phase project spanning three years, covering discovery, IA restructuring, prototyping, and iterative validation.

Context & Problem

Background

PepsiCo’s global intranet is used daily by employees across regions, functions, and business units. Over the years, the platform had become a fragmented ecosystem with hundreds of microsites, inconsistent layouts, and duplicated content. The legacy homepage was especially problematic: it was overloaded with news and promotional content, but none of it helped employees complete their day to day tasks. Key tools and resources were buried, navigation patterns were unclear, and the overall experience didn’t support how people actually worked. With the introduction of a refreshed brand and new communication standards, the intranet needed a redesign that improved usability while aligning with the updated visual direction.

The Challenge

The main challenge was to simplify a complex, content heavy environment and make it easier for employees to find what they needed. This required rethinking the Information Architecture, restructuring the navigation model, and redesigning the mega menu, while also improving the taxonomy across the entire site. The goal was to create a more intuitive, consistent experience that supported different user groups, reduced friction, and aligned with the new brand direction.

Goals & Responsibilities

Primary Goals

  • Improve the Information Architecture to reduce complexity and make content easier to find.
  • Redesign the navigation model and mega menu to support faster, more intuitive access to tools and resources.
  • Refine the sitewide taxonomy to create a consistent structure across hundreds of microsites.
  • Align the intranet with the new brand direction and updated communication standards.
  • Create a more useful homepage that supports employees’ day to day tasks instead of overwhelming them with news.
  • Establish a scalable structure that could support future growth and content governance.

My Responsibilities

  • Reworked the Information Architecture, including navigation flows, content grouping, and hierarchy.
  • Redesigned the mega menu and key navigation patterns to reduce click paths and improve clarity.
  • Refined the taxonomy across the entire site to ensure consistency and easier content discovery.
  • Supported research activities such as card sorting, usability testing, and stakeholder interviews.
  • Created wireframes, interaction patterns, and layout improvements for core pages.
  • Collaborated with Product Managers, Engineering teams, Communications partners, and the brand designer to ensure alignment across design, content, and technical constraints.
  • Contributed to the structure and logic behind components used in the evolving design system.
  • Led daily calls with developers to ensure the build matched the design intent, answering questions, clarifying interactions, and occasionally helping with HTML and CSS when needed.
  • Identified layout and usability issues during development, catching small but impactful improvements that could have been missed due to the team’s workload and tight timelines.

Information Architecture

The intranet’s structure had grown organically over the years, resulting in duplicated content, inconsistent naming, and navigation patterns that didn’t match how employees actually searched for information. My work focused on reorganizing this ecosystem so it could support clearer paths, faster access, and a more intuitive experience across regions and business units. To do this, I restructured the navigation model, regrouped content based on user tasks, and redesigned the mega menu to reduce unnecessary steps. Improving the mega menu also meant revisiting the entire site’s taxonomy, cleaning up labels, aligning terminology across teams, and creating a more consistent hierarchy that could scale as new content was added. I collaborated closely with the UX Architect and content partners to validate these decisions through card sorting, stakeholder reviews, and iterative testing. The result was a more coherent IA that supported both everyday tasks and long term governance, making it easier for employees to find what they needed without getting lost in the complexity of the system.

Reflection

Redesigning such a large intranet helped me refine how I approach complexity. I learned how much impact clear taxonomy and navigation decisions can have on people’s day to day work, and how essential it is to stay aligned with developers during implementation. Supporting them with design clarification, and sometimes even HTML/CSS, showed me how small details can easily get lost when teams are under pressure. Going forward, I’d push even harder for early alignment on content governance and long term maintenance.
